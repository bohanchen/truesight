A 22-YEARS-OLD PhD STUDENT MAJORING IN APPLIED MATH

Q: How long have you been in America so far?

This is my seventh year in America: 3 years in American high school, 3 years as an undergrad and 1 year in Ph.D.


Q: Why do you choose to study your current major?

I am majoring in Math. I like math since I was a little kid, and I am really good at it. I was also on the math team when I was in China. I think math is the most objective, beautiful, and pure subject in the world that can motivate my brain.


Q: What does it feel like to be a Ph.D. student at the age of an undergrad? Why did you make this decision?

I think it is kind of normal to graduate from a math major in three years. Most of the math majors don’t have a master degree, only Ph.D. I got the full scholarship as a math Ph.D. student, so I don’t need to ask moeny from my parents anymore.

Q: Do you think the academic abilities of today’s Chinese students are decreasing in the past few years?

Most of the Chinese students I believe are very strong in academic works. Although not every student was great when I was in high school, a large percentage of people I knew at RPI are great students.


Q: What is the most important value you believe that Chinese students should have?

Be yourself, work hard.


Q: What do you do when you are free?

Playing video games; watching funny and academic videos; reading books

Q: What do you miss most from your hometown?

I miss my friends and the food.


Q: How do you think the negative news of Chinese students?

I generally don’t care. I think they are just some special cases.


Q: Do you think the general public have any misunderstanding of Chinese students who study aboard?

I guess they think we might just be partying every day and the courses are easy. Obviously, this is not true.


Q: Do you want to go back to China or stay in America?

I want to stay in America as I am going to focus on my academic research in the future.
