A HAPPY STUDENT COUPLES

Q: How long have you been in America so far?

Four years


Q: How do you think Chinese students’ love values?

Generally speaking, it is good. I don’t see it as much a difference between students in America and students in China.


Q: Do you think Chinese students’ circle is chaotic?

I think at RPI is ok, it might because we are in a small and technical school. Some individuals might have chaotic and promiscuous life, but this kind of situation is everywhere, and not just restricted to Chinese students.

Q: What is the most important value in a relationship?

It should be properly matched by sharing similar values.


Q: How do you think long-distance relationship?

I think it is really difficult that requires strong trust and love. It also depends on how far the distance is.


Q: What do you miss the most about your hometown

Miss the food and my family


Q: How do you think the negative news of Chinese students?

The media shouldn’t use special cases to cover the entire Chinese students who are studying aboard. A lot of rich kids in China are also a mess. It really depends on the individual.

Q: Do you think the general public have any misunderstanding of Chinese students who study aboard?

They think we are all very rich, whcih is not true, becaus it is getting easier to studying aboard nowadays.


Q: What is the most important value you believe that Chinese students should have?

We should focus on study and have a good life.: travel, embrace different experience and cultures. It might be difficult to have the time and opportunity once I go back to China and start working.


Q: Do you want to go back to China or stay in America?

I want to go back to China. There are a lot of opportunities in the big cities in China. If I say, I want to live in New York City.
